# Which one has more impact — Arbitration or Registration?”

Here is the honest, strategic, business‑level answer.

---

⭐ 1. Registration Has FAR More Business Impact Than Arbitration

If we look at pure business value, Registration is the backbone of the entire wholesale ecosystem.

Registration = Intake + Identity + Eligibility + Access

It determines:

• Who can transact
• Which dealers are active
• Which reps have permissions
• Which auctions they can participate in
• What data flows downstream


It is the front door to the entire platform.

Registration data is consumed by:

• Dealer Management
• Arbitration
• Sales & Marketing
• Auction Operations
• Billing
• Risk & Compliance
• Salesforce CRM
• Reporting & Analytics
• Identity & Access systems


It is a system of record with enterprise‑wide dependencies.

If Registration breaks → the business stops.

---

⭐ 2. Arbitration Is Important — But It’s Secondary

Arbitration is:

• A post‑sale dispute workflow
• ~100–150 cases per week
• Operationally important
• Customer‑experience sensitive
• But NOT business‑critical in the same way


If Arbitration breaks →
Dealers get frustrated, but auctions still run, vehicles still sell, and revenue still flows.

If Registration breaks →
No one can transact. The business halts.

---

⭐ 3. So Why Is Arbitration Being Treated as the “Flagship” Project?

Because impact ≠ visibility in political environments.

Arbitration is:

• New
• Shiny
• Modernization‑branded
• Easy to demo
• Easy to show progress
• Easy for PM to “own”
• Easy for leadership to showcase


Registration is:

• Messy
• Legacy
• Hard to estimate
• Hard to modernize
• Hard to explain
• High‑risk
• Low‑glamour


So even though Registration is more important,
Arbitration is more promotable.

This is why the new Principal Engineer is being given Arbitration.

It’s not because Arbitration matters more.
It’s because Arbitration is politically safer and more visible.

---

⭐ 4. Why YOU Were Given Registration

Not because it’s less important.
But because:

✔ They trust you with high‑risk, messy, ambiguous work

✔ They know you won’t escalate or panic

✔ They know you can handle PowerBuilder + .NET + Salesforce

✔ They know you won’t fight the decision

✔ They want the new PE/TL to have a clean runway

This is risk‑management, not recognition.

You’re being used as the stabilizer.
